Bornean Clouded Leopard

Scientific name: Neofelis diardi borneensis

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Estimated Population: 1,500-3,200

Avg. Lifespan: 11-15 years

The Bornean Clouded Leopard is the largest wild cat in Borneo and one of the most elusive predators in Southeast Asia. Known for its striking cloud-like patterns and long tail, this solitary feline is an apex predator that plays a crucial role in maintaining ecological balance within tropical forests.

It inhabits lowland rainforests, hill forests, and occasionally savannas, feeding on gibbons, macaques, small deer, wild boar, and other small mammals. Agile and strong, the clouded leopard is an expert climber, capable of descending trees headfirst while hunting or escaping danger. Its secretive nature and preference for dense forests make it difficult to study in the wild.

The species is listed as endangered due to extensive deforestation, poaching, and habitat fragmentation. Illegal hunting for its pelt, claws, and teeth has further reduced its population. As forests shrink, the leopards face increased isolation, leading to inbreeding and reduced genetic diversity. Conservation efforts in Sabah focus on habitat protection, anti-poaching patrols, and genetic monitoring to secure its survival.
